1. Mortgage 850: Maximizing Your Home Purchasing Power

There are over 90 different rate and fee adjustments based on credit score and down payment with the most popular mortgages as outlined in this chart from Fannie Mae. When you compare mortgages with the same lenders, you are only comparing the mortgage with one of those 90 possibilities.

Lenders don’t give you a “Oh, you’re close so we will give it to you the better loan”. You either have the required credit score or you do not. Even if you are 1 FICO® Score below, you will not qualify for the better loan. Every point matters!

Source: Fannie Mae

When you apply for a mortgage (or any loan), the lender pulls the Classic FICO Score – say 715. You go to another lender, and they pull the same FICO Score of 715, unless something dramatic has happened in your credit report impacting your score. You go to the third lender, and that lender pulls the same 715 credit score.

If you put 5% down and have a $400,000 mortgage, the loan level price adjustment costs (see chart for LLPAs) associated with that mortgage is $4,500 ($400,000 * .01125 = $4,500) – under 5% down and 715 credit score. Every lender has to pay this $4,500 fee. When you shop your mortgage to multiple lenders, all lenders must pay the same fee – or $4,500 - for that mortgage to the guarantor.



Loan Box

Lenders put your loan into the same loan box, with the same fees impacting your closing costs and interest rate. You are limited in trying to find a lower interest rate and payment because of the loan fess attached from the lender’s backside of the transaction. Loan officers don’t care if you pay these LLPA fees because it is your loan and you are paying these fees. Many don’t even realize these fees are being accessed to you because their respective companies don’t disclose it to their loan officers.

You have to get out of that loan box and reduce those LLPA fees in order to secure the best mortgage. Shopping your mortgage will not reduce these LLPA fees if you have the same credit score.

You can do better. The best option that lenders never discuss is if you can make adjustments to your loan from a higher credit score or down payment and what it does to your purchasing power, interest rate and monthly payment. Mortgage 850 does that with its proprietary technology to help you find the best mortgage loan by finding ways in which you can lower these LLPA fees.

3. Lender’s Credit Report and
3 Classic FICO® Scores


There are 92 different credit scores with 67 of them created by FICO®. All of these scores produce a different 3-digit result. All mortgage lenders utilize the 3 Classic FICO® Scores that have a range of 300 to 850, and most lenders also use these 3 scores for other types of lending including credit cards and auto loans. Of the 92 credit scores, these are the only 3 you should track.

Important: Assessing your lender’s credit report and 3 Classic FICO Scores does not show up as a hard inquiry and drop your credit scores.




The “Consumer-Direct” industry policy prohibits companies form giving you the same credit scores lenders use. For this reason, the credit scores you access from various sources are ones of the nearly 80-non lender credit scores. These credit scores are different from lender’s credit scores. For this reason, lenders do not use that credit score found in the internet, or the ones even offered from your credit card company, bank or credit union.

Only through a non-profit can you access the same credit report and scores lenders utilize.

Until now, counseling organizations have generally been prohibited by their contracts with the credit reporting agencies from giving the consumer the credit report or score that they have purchased on that consumer’s behalf. For example, a nonprofit organization that purchases a credit report with a FICO credit score has typically signed a three-way agreement with one of the three large credit reporting agencies (TransUnion, Equifax, or Experian) and FICO, agreeing not to provide the report or score to any entity, including the consumer.
